Who we are

A not-for-profit growing connection, not just gardens

Seeds for Country is a not-for-profit social enterprise. We sell curated packs of Australian native seeds, supplied by Aboriginal-owned nurseries, and we make sure a real share of every sale flows back to Aboriginal people and Country.

We started because of a simple gap. People want to plant natives and do something good for the land around them, but the usual way of buying seed sends nothing back to the people whose Country those plants come from.

The problem we are working on

Ordinary seed shops are convenient, and often cheap. What they do not offer is provenance, a local match for where you live, or any connection back to Aboriginal people and the land. Your money buys seed and stops there.

We are trying to do it differently. The same act of planting becomes a way to back Aboriginal-owned business and Aboriginal-led care of Country. We show the money trail plainly, so you can always see where your share goes.

Why buy from us

A curated, local native garden that also gives back

We will be honest with you. If the only thing you want is the cheapest seed, a big online shop will usually beat us on price. What we offer is different, and we think it is worth it.

Buying from Seeds for Country

  • + Curation, so you get a sensible set of plants that work together, not guesswork
  • + Local provenance, with species drawn from your own area rather than a national mix
  • + Transparency, with the money trail shown plainly
  • + The give-back, where about half of every pack reaches Aboriginal people and Country
  • + Easy-to-succeed-with seed that is pre-treated, with a plain-language growing guide

A mainstream seed shop

  • + Usually cheaper, and that is a fair point
  • + Wide range, posted anywhere
  • − Sends nothing back to Aboriginal people or Country
  • − Little or no local matching for where you actually live
  • − No real provenance or story behind the seed

We are not trying to say we are unlike anything else. We are saying that if you care about provenance, a local match and giving back, this is a good way to plant.

Our give-back

About half of every pack reaches Aboriginal people and Country

We keep this simple and honest. Roughly half of what you pay for a pack flows back, and it does so as two separate, clear flows rather than a vague promise.

1. A fair payment for work

Paid to the Aboriginal-owned nursery that grows and supplies the seed. This is payment for real work and skill, not charity.

2. A donation to restoration

Given to Aboriginal-led, on-Country restoration, so the planting reaches well beyond your own garden.

As a not-for-profit, we do not exist to make a profit for owners. The majority of any surplus we make goes back into the same work, growing the give-back over time.
